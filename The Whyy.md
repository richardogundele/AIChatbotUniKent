Job Description
AI Software Engineer
Salary: Grade 7
Contract: Full time, fixed term to 28 February 2029
Location: Canterbury Campus
Responsible to: Head of AI
Job family: Administrative, professional and managerial
Job purpose
The Software Engineer (AI Applications) is a delivery-focused role within the AI@Kent team, supporting the
development and implementation of practical AI-enabled tools for students, academic staff and professionalservices teams.
Working under the direction of the Head of AI, and in collaboration with colleagues across the University, the
postholder will contribute to the design, build and refinement of AI applications that address clearly defined user
needs. A key example of this work is the development of AI chatbots that provide safe, accurate and Universityapproved 24/7 responses to common student queries.
The role focuses on the creation, prototyping, implementation and iterative improvement of AI tools, operating
within established technical frameworks, governance arrangements and architectural decisions, rather than setting
them.
Key accountabilities
The following are the main duties for the job. Other duties, commensurate with the grading of the job, may also be
assigned from time to time.
• Develop, test and maintain AI-enabled applications that support learning, teaching, student experience and
professional-services delivery.
• Build and support AI chatbots and assistants using approved large language model (LLM) APIs, ensuring outputs
are accurate, accessible and aligned with University guidance and content.
• Work with academic and professional-services colleagues to understand requirements, contribute to solution
design, and implement agreed functionality.
• Implement retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) patterns where required, using tools, data sources and
approaches agreed at team level.
• Develop backend services, APIs or scripts that integrate AI tools with University systems and content
repositories, following agreed integration patterns.
• Test, document and refine AI tools prior to and during deployment, responding to feedback and resolving issues
within agreed scope.
• Contribute to shared codebases using agreed development practices, including version control, testing and
documentation.
• Support pilots and limited rollouts of AI tools, escalating technical, security or governance issues as appropriate.
• Ensure all work aligns with University policies on data protection, accessibility, security, academic integrity and
responsible use of AI

Key challenges and decisions
The following provide an overview of the most challenging or complex parts of the role and the degree of autonomy
that exists.
• Implementing AI tools that are reliable, usable and safe while working within defined technical and governance
constraints.
• Balancing rapid development and iteration with the need for stability, accessibility and institutional assurance.
• Working across diverse use cases and stakeholder groups while avoiding unnecessary customisation or
fragmentation of solutions.
• Knowing when to escalate design, risk or governance decisions rather than resolving them independently.
Facts & figures
The AI@Kent team will support the adoption of AI technologies and governance across a wide range of academic
programmes, research services and university operations. Kent is positioning itself as a sector leader in the
responsible and educationally grounded use of AI, and the team will play a central role in enabling this ambition.
The University is investing significantly in AI capability, including platform licensing, specialist staffing and digital
transformation projects. The postholder will support the realisation of this investment through the secure
management of Kent’s ChatGPT Edu environment and the development of robust, scalable AI tools that enhance
learning, teaching, research and professional-services delivery.
Internal & external relationships
Internal: Head of AI (reporting line), Lead AI Platform Engineer, AI and Education Specialists, AI Training Specialist -
Professional Services teams including Information Technology Services (ITS), Technology Enhanced Learning (TEL),
Library, Student Success, and Planning, Insights and Improvements - Information Governance and Legal Compliance -
Quality Assurance, Research & Innovation Services and Student Experience – relevant working groups, and internal
policy leads
External: Occasional interaction with external AI tool providers or developer communities, under the direction of
the Head of AI.
